# Project Vision

## What is Internal Sea?

Internal Sea is an internal operating platform that connects how we describe business data, how we deliver work, and how we organize people and capabilities. Instead of three disconnected tools, teams get one system where data products, delivery tasks, ownership, risks, decisions and technical debt are linked and visible.

## Why we are building it

Organizations often split these concerns across spreadsheets, Jira, catalog tools and org charts. That fragmentation makes it hard to answer simple questions:

- Who owns this data product?
- What work is blocked because of a capability gap?
- Which teams depend on a dataset that is at risk?
- Where is technical debt tied to a business domain?

Internal Sea exists to give business and delivery teams a shared, trustworthy view of products, work and people — with room to grow into search, activity feeds and AI assistance later.

## Internal operating system

Internal Sea is **not only** a data catalog, **not only** a task manager, and **not only** a team directory. It is an internal operating system that connects:

- **Products and catalog assets** — data products, dashboards, reports, datasets
- **Delivery and work** — projects, work items, risks, decisions, meetings
- **People and organization** — teams, capabilities, skills, allocations
- **Performance** — utilization, velocity, quality, delivery health
- **Compliance and governance** — policies, rules, controls, evidence
- **Tools and automation** — integrations, triggers, schedules
- **Commercial pipeline** — deals, POCs, pilots, MVPs becoming projects

The **relationship layer** between these objects is the core differentiator — a dashboard links to a data product, a project links to compliance checks, a deal links to a future internal project.

## Main user groups

| User group | Primary needs |
|------------|---------------|
| **Business users** | Find and understand data products, domains and ownership |
| **Product owners** | Prioritize work, link delivery to products and outcomes |
| **Data owners** | Govern datasets, document quality, risks and decisions |
| **Delivery teams** | Manage tasks, track progress, see dependencies |
| **Capability leads** | Map skills, teams and allocations to demand |
| **Leadership** | Dashboards on ownership, delivery health and capability gaps |

## Core modules

### Data Product Catalog

Business-facing registry of data products: what they are, who owns them, which domain they belong to, and how they relate to work and risks.

### Work Management

Jira-like work items for delivery: tasks, status, assignees, links to data products and related entities.

### Team and Capability Management

People, teams, skills, capabilities and allocations — so demand and capacity are visible alongside delivery and catalog data.

### Relationship Layer

Cross-cutting links between products, work, people, teams, risks, decisions and technical debt. This layer is what makes the platform more than three siloed modules.

## Product principles

1. **Business-first** — Labels, screens and data should make sense to business users, not only engineers.
2. **Connected work** — Work items, products and people should reference each other by design.
3. **Ownership by design** — Every important object should have a clear owner or steward.
4. **Capability visibility** — Skills and team capacity should be visible where planning happens.
5. **AI-ready later** — Structure data and docs so summarization and assistants can be added without rework.

## Success criteria (long term)

- A user can find a data product and see related work, owners and risks in one place.
- Delivery teams can manage work without losing the link to business context.
- Capability leads can see who is allocated where and what skills exist on teams.
- Leadership can trust dashboards built on consistent relationships, not manual exports.
